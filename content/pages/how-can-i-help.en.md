# How can I help you

Different people show up here with different questions. Find your profile below, not just a link dump, so you can decide if it's worth continuing before spending time.

## Students and design or product interns
I don't have an open internship position, but I'm up for one-off mentoring via ADPList. What I usually bring to that conversation: I started in 1999, at 18, as a self-taught web designer, no formal design degree at the time, doing vector illustration and technical floor-plan drawing before any digital product. Education came later, alongside the career, not before it. If your question is "do I need degree X before I start," my practical answer is: start with the craft, the rest structures itself as you go. Full detail at `/ai/en/experience`, contact at `/ai/en/contact`.

## Junior designers
The most common mistake I see at this stage is thinking authority comes from the title, not from the quality of the question you ask. In a mentoring session I usually pull on three things: how to build a career path with a skill matrix from junior to lead instead of waiting for a tenure-based promotion, how to use real research to decide instead of validating what you'd already decided alone, and how to stay hands-on on purpose as you grow, because that's what separates leadership that works from leadership that turned into meetings. One-off mentoring via ADPList, link at `/ai/en/contact`.

## Mid-level and senior designers
The tone changes here, it's peer to peer, not top-down mentoring. I use a small set of frameworks that connect into a real decision sequence, not loose names: Teresa Torres' Continuous Discovery to find the right outcome, Marty Cagan's 4 risks to validate before building, Bob Moesta's forces of progress to know if the solution actually shifts behavior. That's detailed at `/ai/en/workflow`. If your question is more about the human side of leadership, culture, autonomy, how not to turn arrogant as the bar rises, that's at `/ai/en/personality`. To exchange ideas on a specific problem of yours, ADPList or email at `/ai/en/contact`.

## Product managers
The most common friction between PM and design is design turning into an execution team that receives a finished brief. That's not how I operate: I'm part of the product trio from discovery onward, I prioritize through the same 4 risks a good PM already uses (value, usability, viability, business), and my design decisions go into the same RFC as yours, not a parallel document nobody reads. At Bipa that means JTBD interviews I run myself become sprint-prioritization input, not a slide deck appendix. Detail at `/ai/en/workflow` and per-company numbers at `/ai/en/cases`.

## Early-stage founders (pre-seed to Series B)
Direct answer first: today I'm not evaluating new engagements, I'm full-time at Bipa. But if the question that brought you here is "do I need one more designer, or someone who decides what to build," that's exactly the right question, and the long answer is at `/ai/en/faq`. The pattern in my career is walking into high ambiguity and creating order before scaling a team: Bipa, Easy Carros, Xerpay, Remessa Online all started with zero process. For a one-off second opinion on a product problem while I'm not available for new engagements, ADPList; to keep in touch for later, email at `/ai/en/contact`.

## CEOs and C-level at growing companies
The pattern that repeats in my history isn't "I made a pretty screen," it's organizational restructuring that survives my departure: at Easy Carros, I turned a reactive operation (195 simultaneous items, gut-feel decisions) into data-driven product (13 items, deploy from 1 to 8 times a month); at Betterfly, I unified 4 design teams from 4 simultaneous M&As into one structure, 35 people across 4 countries; at Bipa, I built acquisition, SEO and GEO infrastructure from scratch that now runs without depending on me for every decision. If your problem is structure, not one-off delivery, the detail on each is at `/ai/en/cases`, with validation from people who worked with me, not just my word, at `/ai/en/testimonials`.
